Discover Pyin Oo Lwin

Once a British retreat, this picturesque hill station offers a refreshing escape from Myanmar's tropical heat

Pyin Oo Lwin (formerly Maymyo) is a picturesque hill station in Myanmar's Mandalay Region, known for its cool climate, colonial architecture, and lush gardens. Nestled in the Shan Highlands, it's a refreshing escape where British colonial charm meets the natural beauty of Myanmar.

With its vibrant flower gardens, strawberry farms, scenic waterfalls, and cool mountain air, this peaceful town has become a beloved retreat. Wander through pine forests, admire colonial mansions, ride a horse-drawn carriage, and sip fresh coffee in a garden café.

Top Attractions

Explore the must-see sights and experiences in Pyin Oo Lwin

National Kandawgyi Gardens

A stunning botanical garden with exotic plants, a butterfly museum, and serene lakes. Perfect for nature lovers and photographers.

Purcell Tower

A British-era landmark resembling Big Ben, gifted by Queen Victoria. The clock tower is a symbol of Pyin Oo Lwin's colonial heritage.

Pwe Kauk Waterfalls

A scenic waterfall surrounded by pine forests, perfect for picnics and nature walks. The cool mist provides relief from the tropical heat.

Peik Chin Myaung Cave

A mystical limestone cave filled with Buddhist shrines and natural formations. The cave extends about 150 meters with stunning stalactites.

Colonial Architecture

Well-preserved British-era buildings, including churches and cottages. Take a walking tour to admire the unique architectural heritage.

Local Markets

Vibrant markets offering fresh produce, flowers, and local crafts. Don't miss the famous coffee and strawberry products.

Travel Information

Everything you need to plan your visit to Pyin Oo Lwin

How to Get There

  • From Mandalay: Shared taxis, buses, or motorbike (~1.5–2 hours)
  • Train: A slow but scenic 4-hour journey from Mandalay
  • Private Car: Most comfortable option with hotel pickup available
  • Bicycle: Adventurous option for experienced cyclists

Where to Stay

  • Budget: Aureum Hotel, Grace Hotel (~$20-40/night)
  • Mid-Range: Kandawgyi Hill Resort (~$50-100/night)
  • Luxury: The Governor's House (colonial-style boutique hotel, ~$150+/night)
  • Unique: Colonial-era guesthouses and homestays

Food & Drink

  • Local Specialties: Strawberry dishes, avocado salads, Shan noodles
  • Must-Try: Pyin Oo Lwin coffee and fresh dairy products
  • Best Restaurants: Pan Taw Win Cafe & Restaurant
  • Markets: Morning market for fresh produce and snacks
